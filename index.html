Best Time to Visit Pakistan

Northern Pakistan is a four-season destination. Each season transforms these valleys into an entirely different paradise. Here is your complete seasonal guide.

🌸
Spring
April — May
5°C to 18°C

Cherry blossom season in Hunza — the valley turns breathtaking pink and white. Fewer crowds, pleasant weather, and roads begin to open after winter closure.

Best For: 🌸 Hunza · Swat · Naran
☀️
Summer
June — August
10°C to 25°C

Peak tourist season. All routes are open, including Babusar Pass and Khunjerab Pass. Lush green meadows, clear mountain views and flowing waterfalls everywhere. Book early!

Best For: ☀️ All 5 Destinations
🍁
Autumn
Sep — October
2°C to 15°C

Stunning golden and amber foliage across Hunza and Naran. Fewer crowds than summer, crisp clean air and some of the most dramatic landscape colours of the year.

Best For: 🍁 Hunza · Skardu · Neelum
❄️
Winter
Nov — March
−12°C to 5°C

Snow-covered villages, skiing at Malam Jabba (Swat), and magical frozen landscapes. Kalam and Swat remain accessible year-round. Perfect for winter adventure lovers.

Best For: ❄️ Swat · Kalam · Murree
Why Travel Pakistan

The World's Most Underrated Adventure

Pakistan is home to more mountains above 7,000 metres than any other country on Earth. With five of the world's 14 eight-thousanders, 108 peaks above 7,000m, and thousands of glaciers, it is a destination that humbles every traveller who visits.

Beyond the mountains — warm hospitality, ancient civilisations, vibrant bazaars and unforgettable flavours await you, all at a fraction of the cost of comparable international destinations.

Baltit Fort, Hunza — 700 Years of History

Perched high above Karimabad, this ancient fort has guarded Hunza for over 700 years. Restored by the Aga Khan Trust for Culture, it offers panoramic views of Rakaposhi peak (7,788m) and the entire Hunza Valley. A UNESCO World Heritage candidate site and must-visit landmark.

💧

Attabad Lake, Hunza — Nature's Turquoise Jewel

Formed by a massive 2010 landslide, Attabad Lake's brilliant turquoise waters stretch 21 km along the Karakoram Highway. Visitors can boat, kayak, or simply sit in awe of one of Asia's most dramatic and beautiful natural formations — impossible to photograph without gasping.

🏜

Sarfaranga Cold Desert, Skardu — 2,300m High

Pakistan's only high-altitude cold desert sits near Skardu, surrounded by towering snow-capped peaks on all sides. This unique and surreal landscape — golden dunes set against icy mountains — feels like another planet and is unlike anything else in South Asia.

🌅

Deosai National Park — World's 2nd Highest Plateau

"The Land of Giants" sits at 4,114m and spans 3,000 sq km of untouched wilderness. In summer it is carpeted with wildflowers and home to the rare Himalayan Brown Bear, Snow Leopard and golden eagles. The horizon stretches endlessly across one of Earth's most awe-inspiring landscapes.
