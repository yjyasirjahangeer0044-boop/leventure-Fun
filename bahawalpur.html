About Gwadar

The Pearl of
the Arabian Sea

Gwadar is Pakistan's most exciting emerging destination — a coastal city on the Balochistan coast where the Arabian Sea meets dramatic Makran mountains. As the centrepiece of the China-Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC), it is transforming into a world-class deep-sea port city.

Beyond CPEC, Gwadar has always been a natural paradise. Golden beaches, giant rock formations shaped like hammers and sphinxes, active mud volcanoes, and Pakistan's largest offshore island are all within reach. The seafood here — fresh from the Arabian Sea every morning — is among the best in the country.

🏖 Hammerhead Peninsula

Naturally formed peninsula surrounded by sea on 3 sides — Gwadar's most iconic landmark

🦭 Astola Island

Pakistan's largest offshore island — 40km from Pasni, famous for scuba diving and dolphins

🌋 Mud Volcanoes

Chandragup sacred mud volcano — erupts mud not lava, revered by Hindu pilgrims

🦁 Princess of Hope

Natural rock statue named by Angelina Jolie during a UN visit to Pakistan

Top Places to Visit

Six Wonders of Bahawalpur

01
858 AD · Cholistan Desert
Derawar Fort
Pakistan's most magnificent desert fortress — 40 circular bastions 30m high, visible for miles across Cholistan. Built in 858 AD by Rajput ruler Rai Jajja Bhati, renovated 1732.
02
1872 · Bahawalpur City
Noor Mahal Palace
Elegant Italian-style palace built in 1872 by Nawab Sir Sadiq Muhammad Khan. Now a Pakistan Army museum with family portraits, period weapons and regal memorabilia. Spectacular at night.
03
13th Century · UNESCO Nominee
Uch Sharif
Ancient Sufi city 70km from Derawar — remarkable 13th-century shrines with intricate tilework and carved minarets. One of Pakistan's most spiritual and architecturally rich heritage sites.
04
Abbasi Necropolis
Royal Abbasi Graveyard
120 ornately carved marble tombs of Bahawalpur's ruling family near Derawar — beautifully decorated with floral patterns and Quranic calligraphy. Mughal and Persian influences.
05
UNESCO Biosphere · 650 km²
Lal Suhanra National Park
UNESCO Biosphere Reserve 35km east of Bahawalpur — chinkara deer, endangered blackbuck, wild boar and rich migratory birds. Jeep safaris and guided nature walks available.
06
Annual Event · 70,000+ Visitors
Cholistan Jeep Rally
Pakistan's most exciting motorsport event — held annually near Derawar Fort. 70,000+ spectators from across Pakistan and abroad. TDCP-organised, part of the international rally calendar.

Practical Information

🚂 How to Get There

  • Train: Lahore → Bahawalpur (~5.5hrs), multiple daily
  • Bus: Lahore → Bahawalpur via M4 motorway (~4hrs)
  • Multan → Bahawalpur by road (~1.5hrs)
  • Nearest airport: Bahawalpur Airport (limited flights)

🏨 Where to Stay

  • Serena Hotel Bahawalpur — premier, heritage style
  • Hotel One Bahawalpur — modern, central
  • Desert Camp near Derawar — unique overnight
  • Guesthouses in Ahmedpur East (near Derawar)

⚠️ Important Tips

  • Best Oct–Feb — summer temp exceeds 45°C, avoid
  • 4x4 jeep essential for Derawar — 3-4hr drive off-road
  • Hire a local guide for Derawar — fort permission needed
  • Bring sunscreen, hat and 3L water per person

💱 For International Visitors

  • USD accepted at Serena Hotel and exchange shops
  • ATMs available in Bahawalpur city only
  • 1 USD ≈ 280 PKR (March 2026)
