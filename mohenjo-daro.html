About Gwadar

The Pearl of
the Arabian Sea

Gwadar is Pakistan's most exciting emerging destination — a coastal city on the Balochistan coast where the Arabian Sea meets dramatic Makran mountains. As the centrepiece of the China-Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC), it is transforming into a world-class deep-sea port city.

Beyond CPEC, Gwadar has always been a natural paradise. Golden beaches, giant rock formations shaped like hammers and sphinxes, active mud volcanoes, and Pakistan's largest offshore island are all within reach. The seafood here — fresh from the Arabian Sea every morning — is among the best in the country.

🏖 Hammerhead Peninsula

Naturally formed peninsula surrounded by sea on 3 sides — Gwadar's most iconic landmark

🦭 Astola Island

Pakistan's largest offshore island — 40km from Pasni, famous for scuba diving and dolphins

🌋 Mud Volcanoes

Chandragup sacred mud volcano — erupts mud not lava, revered by Hindu pilgrims

🦁 Princess of Hope

Natural rock statue named by Angelina Jolie during a UN visit to Pakistan

Six Wonders of Mohenjo-daro

01
2500 BCE · World's First
The Great Bath
World's earliest known public water tank — a watertight 12x7m brick pool built 4,500 years ago. No European city would build anything comparable for another 3,000 years.
02
Urban Planning · 2500 BCE
The Grid-Pattern Streets
Remarkably regular street grid — 9m wide main roads, 1.5m lanes, all running north-south and east-west. Brick-paved, with covered drainage under every street. 5,000 years ahead of their time.
03
Civic Architecture
The Great Granary
Massive public grain storage complex — evidence of state-level food management and redistribution system. One of the largest structures in the ancient world of its time.
04
Bronze Age · Artefact
Mohenjo-daro Museum
On-site museum housing the famous Priest-King bust, Dancing Girl bronze replica, elephant and rhinoceros seals, pottery, jewellery and remarkable civic planning artefacts.
05
Buddhist Era · 2nd Century
The Buddhist Stupa
Later Buddhist stupa built over the Indus Valley ruins — visible from across the site. First excavated in 1922. The highest point and most recognisable structure at Mohenjo-daro.
06
Sindhi Culture · Sufi Heritage
Sehwan & Sindh Culture
Combine your Mohenjo-daro visit with Sehwan Sharif — shrine of Lal Shahbaz Qalandar. Manchar Lake (Asia's largest fresh water lake) and the ancient bazaars of Larkana city.

Sindh Experiences

🏛
Site Photography
Golden hour at Mohenjo-daro — brick ruins glowing in warm light. International photographers visit specifically for this.
🔬
Archaeological Deep-Dive
All five excavation zones with expert guide — drainage systems, assembly hall, priest's college and residential blocks.
🎶
Qawwali at Sehwan
Thursday night dhol and qawwali at Lal Shahbaz Qalandar's shrine — one of Pakistan's most electric spiritual experiences.
⛵
Manchar Lake Boat
Asia's largest freshwater lake — fishing communities live on wooden boats. Sunset boat ride through the reeds.
🐬
Indus Dolphin Watch
Rare blind Indus River dolphins can be spotted near Sukkur — one of the world's most endangered river dolphin species.
🧵
Sindhi Craft Shopping
Ajrak block-printed cloth, Sindhi embroidery, tarkashi metalwork and lacquerware — unique Pakistan crafts.
🍛
Sindhi Food Trail
Sindhi biryani, sai bhaji, fresh Indus fish, seyal maas and the famous Sukkur halwa — authentic flavours.
🌙
Desert Stargazing
Sindh's flat landscape and low light pollution make it exceptional for stargazing — clear skies November to February.

Practical Information

🚂 How to Get There

  • Fly Karachi → Sukkur (1hr) then drive 2hrs to site
  • Road from Karachi: N-55 Indus Highway (~7hrs, 510km)
  • Train Karachi → Larkana (Mohenjo-daro nearest town)
  • International: fly Karachi, connect via Sukkur airport

🏨 Where to Stay

  • RT Grace Hotel Larkana — closest quality hotel
  • Archaeological Guest House — on-site at Mohenjo-daro
  • Larkana city guesthouses — basic but comfortable
  • Sukkur hotels for extended Sindh stays

⚠️ Important Tips

  • Visit November–February only — summer is dangerously hot
  • Wear proper walking shoes — 3km+ of walking required
  • Carry water, hat and sunscreen — site has no shade
  • International visitors require armed escort (arranged by guide)

💱 For International Visitors

  • USD accepted at Sukkur and Larkana major hotels
  • Entry fee: $5 USD for foreign nationals (PKR 500 local)
  • 1 USD ≈ 280 PKR (March 2026)
